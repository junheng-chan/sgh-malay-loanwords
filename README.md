# Consonant Correspondences of Malay Loanwords in Singaporean Hokkien

Supplementary webpage for the poster **“Consonant Correspondences of Malay Loanwords in Singaporean Hokkien”**, presented at the workshop **“When Chinese Meets Southeast Asian Languages: Two Millennia of Continuous Language Contact.”**

**Authors:** Chan Junheng and Nur Amirah Hakim Binte Mustapha Kamal  
**Workshop:** When Chinese Meets Southeast Asian Languages: Two Millennia of Continuous Language Contact  
**Venue:** National University of Singapore  
**Date:** 2 June 2026

This page presents selected audio clips and spectrograms from a preliminary study of how Malay consonants are realised in Singaporean Hokkien loanwords. The examples illustrate direct correspondences, adaptive mappings, variable realisations, and cases requiring further analysis.

## Poster summary

Malay and Hokkien have been in long-term contact across Southeast Asia. This project examines how Malay consonants are adapted in contemporary Singaporean Hokkien loanwords, using spoken data from Singaporean Hokkien speakers.

## Categories

- **Direct:** the Malay consonant has a close equivalent in Singaporean Hokkien.
- **Adaptive:** the Malay consonant is adapted to a nearby Singaporean Hokkien category.
- **Variable:** different speakers or tokens show different realisations.
- **Uncertain:** the token requires further analysis.

## Data note

The dataset is preliminary and selected examples may be revised as analysis continues. Speaker labels are anonymised.

Audio clips contain speaker voices and should only be shared where permitted by consent.
